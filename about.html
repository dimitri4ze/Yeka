About Yeka Onka

Yeka Onka is a gospel musician and recording artist based in the U.S., known for her dynamic vocal range and unique ability to bridge age and cultural divides, pointing listeners to Christ. Her music explores themes of God's love, faith, and hope.

Born in Eastern Nigeria to musician parents, Yeka grew up surrounded by music, singing in churches and crusades. Without formal musical training, she honed her vocal skills by following her parents' paths and was influenced by artists like Onyeka Onwenu and Whitney Houston.

Yeka's distinctive sound combines energetic praise and intense worship, reflecting her deep love for God. She first gained prominence as the inaugural winner of Nigerian Idol in 2011.

After a personal hiatus, she returned in 2022 with the single "I Will Not Fear," which garnered 1.6 million YouTube views and testimonies of deliverance from depression and suicidal thoughts.

Yeka is now working on her debut album, featuring tracks like "Odim Ebube" and "Jehovah Rohi," expressing her gratitude to Christ.
